PenCom issues guideline on funding equity contributions for Mortgages by Retirement Savings Account Holders

The National Pension Commission (PenCom) has released a guideline on how Retirement Savings Account (RSA) holders could access their RSA balance to fund equity contributions for residential mortgages. The approval gives effect to the provision of Section 89(2) of the Pension Reform Act, 2014 (PRA) which allows RSA holders to use a portion of their RSA balance for payment of equity for a residential mortgage.
The maximum amount that could be accessed for this purpose is 25% of the total mandatory RSA balance as of the date of application, irrespective of the value of equity contribution required by the mortgage lender. Further, RSA holders who are married are eligible to make a joint application.

The approval covers RSA holders in active employment as either salaried employees or self-employed. The guideline provides the following conditions, amongst others:
•  Availability of a duly signed and verified offer letter for the property.
•  Contributions to the Pension Scheme must have been for a minimum period of 5 years.
•  Applicant must not be within the last three years of active service prior to retirement.
•  Mortgage lender and institution must be duly licensed by the Central Bank of Nigeria and be in compliance with the Contributory Pension Scheme
Interested RSA holders may contact their Pension Fund Administrators for more information and guidance.
